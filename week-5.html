What to Bring and What to Leave Behind

Allowed

  • CPT (AMA Professional Edition), ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II -- print or eBook
  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Clear, unlabeled water bottle or cup of water
  • Confirmation/admission ticket (testing center only)
  • Prescription eyeglasses if needed

Not Allowed

  • Scratch paper, whiteboards, or writing utensils
  • Cell phones, tablets, smartwatches, or any electronic devices
  • Food or drinks other than a clear water container
  • Notes, study guides, or loose papers of any kind
  • Physical calculator (online calculator provided)

Arriving and Checking In

  • Testing center: Arrive at least 30 minutes early. Bring your printed confirmation and photo ID. You will go through a security check and store belongings in a provided locker.
  • Live remote: Log in 15 to 30 minutes early. Your proctor will verify your ID, conduct a 360-degree room scan, and review rules before starting.
  • Your name on your photo ID must match your AAPC account exactly. Contact AAPC at least 48 hours before your exam if there is a discrepancy.

Pacing Yourself: The Two-Pass Strategy

You have 4 hours for 100 questions -- about 2.4 minutes each. Use this strategy:

Pass 1: Move Through All Questions

  • Answer what you know confidently and quickly.
  • Flag anything that requires extended lookup or feels uncertain.
  • Do not spend more than 2 minutes on any single question in this pass.

Pass 2: Return to Flagged Questions

  • Use your remaining time for flagged questions.
  • Prioritize questions you feel closest to answering correctly.
  • Save the 10 case studies for last -- they take more time but are worth the same 1 point each.
Answer every question. There is no penalty for guessing. A blank answer is a guaranteed zero; your best guess gives you a chance. The clock does not stop for breaks -- if you need one, keep it short.

If You Do Not Pass the First Time

  • Results are posted in your AAPC account within 7 to 10 business days with a general breakdown by content area.
  • Review the areas of study results to identify where to focus before your second attempt.
  • Your second attempt must be scheduled before your voucher expiration in January 2027.
  • The first-time pass rate is approximately 50%. Many successful coders passed on their second attempt. Do not be discouraged.
This Week's Tasks: Build your personal exam day checklist using this week's handout. Then set a timer for 24 minutes and work through 10 practice questions from your AAPC CPC Study Guide using your code books -- that mirrors the 2.4-minute-per-question pace of the real exam.
