Tabbing Your Physical Books

Tabs are one of the highest-return investments you can make before exam day. The goal is to locate any section within seconds. Rules and best practices:

  • Tabs may be inserted, taped, pasted, glued, or stapled into your books. The intent must clearly be to mark a page, not add information.
  • Write section names or code ranges on tabs so you can identify them at a glance without opening the book.
  • Do not use tabs to add supplemental information or notes that do not relate to coding work.
  • Keep tabs consistent across your three books so your eyes know where to look.

Suggested Tab Locations

BookTab These Locations
CPTEach surgical section (10000s through 60000s), Radiology, Pathology/Lab, Medicine, E/M, Anesthesia, Appendix A (modifiers)
ICD-10-CMOfficial Coding Guidelines, each chapter (1 through 22), Table of Drugs and Chemicals, External Cause Index
HCPCS Level IIIndex, Table of Drugs, each letter section (A through V), Modifiers

Annotations: What Is and Is Not Allowed

Allowed

  • Handwritten notes and annotations in the margins
  • Modifier rules near the modifier appendix
  • Bundling reminders near relevant sections
  • Sequencing notes near guideline sections
  • Guideline summaries in your own words

Not Allowed

  • Long passages of information on blank pages
  • White-out, paint, or printed overlays on pages
  • Questions from study guides or practice exams
  • Loose papers, sticky notes that cover text
  • Content unrelated to coding activities

A Note on eBooks

As of January 2025, AAPC now permits eBooks during the CPC exam. If you purchase eBooks in the future:

  • eBooks are accessed through the AAPC platform and must be selected as your format when you register.
  • You can change your book format selection up to 48 hours before your exam.
  • eBooks and print books can be used together, but only one copy of each book is allowed.
  • eBooks cannot be tabbed the same way physical books can. Search functionality replaces some of the speed advantage tabbing provides.

Study Strategy Tips

  • Study from the same books you will bring to the exam. Familiarity with your specific edition matters.
  • Practice locating codes under timed conditions. Aim to find any section in 15 to 30 seconds.
  • Focus extra time on surgical CPT coding, ICD-10-CM guidelines, and E/M -- the highest-weighted areas.
  • Use the AAPC CPC Study Guide alongside your code books. Do not rely on code books alone for content review.
  • Take at least one full-length timed practice exam before your scheduled test date.
