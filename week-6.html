When You Pass: Your CPC-A Credential

When you pass the CPC exam, you will initially receive the CPC-A (Apprentice) designation. This is standard for anyone who has passed the exam but has not yet verified two years of professional coding experience. The CPC-A is a fully recognized credential -- it simply indicates you are earlier in your career.

  • Results are posted in your AAPC account within 7 to 10 business days.
  • Your digital CPC-A certificate and updated member profile will reflect your new credential once results are released.
  • You will need to release your results to your instructor through your AAPC student account.

Removing the A: CPC-A to CPC

There are three pathways to remove the apprentice designation. Because you are completing an AAPC-approved coding course, you already have a significant head start.

PathwayRequirements
Option 1: Work Experience Only2 years of verified on-the-job coding experience using CPT, ICD-10-CM, or HCPCS. Two letters of verification required.
Option 2: Course + ExperienceYour AAPC-approved coding course counts as one year. Add 1 year of verified work experience to meet the full requirement.
Option 3: Course + PracticodeYour coding course plus completion of AAPC's Practicode program (600 cases at 70% or higher). No additional work experience required.

Processing takes 2 to 4 weeks after submission. Visit aapc.com to begin the application when you are ready.

Maintaining Your Credential: CEUs

Once credentialed, you must earn 36 Continuing Education Units (CEUs) every two years to keep your CPC active. At least 66% must come from core coding content (Curriculum A). Ways to earn CEUs:

Free or Low Cost

  • AAPC local chapter meetings
  • 4 free AAPC webinars per year (1 CEU each)
  • AAPC the Magazine Test Yourself section (1 CEU per issue)
  • CMS web-based training at cms.gov

Paid Options

  • AAPC HealthCon national conference (15+ CEUs)
  • AAPC webinars and online courses
  • Employer-sponsored in-house training
  • AAPC specialty credential courses

Track your CEUs in your AAPC account under Certifications and CEUs. Do not wait until the end of your renewal period to start accumulating them.

Using Your Membership for Your Career

  • AAPC Job Board: Search for coding positions filtered by specialty, location, and experience level at aapc.com/career-center.
  • Salary data: AAPC publishes an annual salary survey. Use it to understand market rates before salary negotiations.
  • Specialty credentials: After gaining experience, consider adding a COC (outpatient facility), CIC (inpatient), or payer-specific credential to increase your marketability.
  • Local chapters: Chapter involvement demonstrates professional commitment and builds the network that leads to job referrals.
  • AAPC community forums: Connect with thousands of credentialed coders for coding questions, career advice, and peer support at aapc.com.
This Week's Tasks: Explore the AAPC Career Center and browse job postings in your area or a specialty you are interested in. Then review the three CPC-A removal pathways and identify which one applies to your current situation.
