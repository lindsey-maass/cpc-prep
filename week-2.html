Books AllowedYes. Open-book. Print or eBook. CPT must be AMA Professional Edition.
ResultsPosted in your AAPC account within 7 to 10 business days

It Is Open Book. That Does Not Make It Easy.

You are allowed to bring your CPT, ICD-10-CM, and HCPCS Level II coding manuals into the exam. However, with only about 2.4 minutes per question, there is no time to look everything up from scratch. Students who pass know their books well enough to find answers quickly. Tabbing and organizing your manuals is one of the most important things you can do -- covered in Part 4 of this series.

Important Things to Know

  • No scratch paper, whiteboards, or writing utensils are permitted. Notes must be written directly in your code books and must relate to coding activities.
  • An online calculator is provided within the testing platform. Physical calculators are not permitted.
  • A clear, unlabeled water bottle or cup of water is allowed.
  • Current-year code books are strongly recommended.
  • AAPC has announced that some exam cases will eventually shift to fill-in-the-blank format. This is already reflected in AAPC practice exams.
